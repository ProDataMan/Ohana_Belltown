# Ohana Belltown — Visual Design Direction

The current site uses a generic "restaurant amber" palette. Ohana already has
a real mark: the neon sign out front, magenta and violet against a dark
Belltown night, next to the black-and-white mon crest logo. This document
proposes building the site's visual identity from that instead of a stock
"modern restaurant" template.

Live mockup: see the "Visual Direction" section of the
[site roadmap artifact](https://claude.ai/code/artifact/4cb3cf12-2e8f-4521-af05-a8a447731e1c)
for a rendered preview of the hero and menu card treatments described below.

## Palette

| Name | Hex | Usage |
|---|---|---|
| Neon Pink | `#FF2F8F` | Primary accent — buttons, links, category dividers, gradient starts |
| Neon Violet | `#8B5CF6` | Secondary accent — pairs with pink in gradients, hover states |
| Sunset Gold | `#F2A93C` | Tertiary accent — prices, "popular"/"new" tags; ties back to the site's current look |
| Belltown Ink | `#120E18` | Dark ground for hero banners, header/nav, footer |
| Warm Paper | `#FAF6F3` | Light ground for long-form content (menu, about, contact) |

Long menu-scanning pages stay on the warm paper ground for legibility — the
neon treatment is reserved for hero banners, buttons, and dividers, so
scanning a 118-item menu doesn't mean reading white text on black for ten
minutes.

## Typography

| Role | Stack | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Display (headings, eyebrows, buttons) | `-apple-system, "SF Pro Display", "Segoe UI", system-ui, sans-serif` | Bold (800), tight tracking at large sizes; wide tracking + small caps for eyebrow labels |
| Body (paragraphs, descriptions) | `Georgia, "Iowan Old Style", "Times New Roman", serif` | Comfortable reading size (~17px), gives long-form pages an editorial warmth instead of a generic SaaS-doc feel |
| Utility (tags, badges, small labels) | `ui-monospace, "SF Mono", Menlo, monospace` | Uppercase, wide tracking, small size |

## Layout Principles

- Dark neon-gradient hero banners on the homepage and section pages, using
  the actual logo and photography, not stock imagery.
- Warm paper background for menu, about, contact, and other text-heavy pages
  so long lists of dishes stay easy to scan.
- Gold reserved for prices and short callout tags so it reads as a signal,
  not a third competing brand color.
- Category dividers and buttons use the pink-to-violet gradient as the one
  bold, recognizable brand element — everything else stays quiet around it.

## Rationale

The pink-to-violet gradient is lifted directly from the storefront photo, so
the site reads as *this specific restaurant* at a glance, not a template.
This is the difference between "a modern restaurant website" and "Ohana
Belltown's website" — the goal is the latter.
